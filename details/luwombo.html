Luwombo

Prep Time:15-20mins
Cook Time:40-50mins(steaming/wrapping step can vary and sauce preparation)
Servings:4 people

The Story Behind the Dish

A classic Ugandan dish where meat or fish(often beef or chicken) is stewed with groundnut sauce, then wrapped and steamed in banana leaves along with matooke(plantains) or served alongside matooke.

Ingredients

  • 3 green plntains(matooke), peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup finely ground unsalted mashed peanuts(groundnuts)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 thumb-size piece of ginger,minced
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped(optional)
  • Water(for cooking sauce and steaming),black pepper, salt for taste
  • Banana leaves(for wrapping;optional alternative is foil but banana leaves give authentic flavor.)
  • Palm oil or cooking oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step1:Prepare the banana leaves by warming them to make them pliable ,if using.
  2. Step2:Boil or steam the matooke plantains until soft; set aside.
  3. Step3:In a pot, mix mashed groundnuts with water to create a creamy sauce.
  4. Step4:Add the sliced onions,minced garlic,ginger,chopped tomatoes and carrots to the sauce.
  5. Step5:Season with salt and pepper, then simmer gently until the sauce thickens.
  6. Step6:Lay out a banana leaf and place a portion of the cooked matooke in the centre.
  7. Step7:Pour the groundnut sauce over the matooke.
  8. Step8:Wrap the banana leaf around the mixture to form a secure parcel.
  9. Step9:Steam the parcels over boiling water for about 30-40 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  10. Step10:Remove from heat and serve hot as hearty and flavorful main dish.
