Mugoyo

Prep Time:15mins for initial preparation(soaking or rinsing beans, preparing the potatoes)
Cook Time:1.5-2 hours(primarily to boil the dried beans until tender and steam the potatoes)
Servings:4-6 people

The Story Behind the Dish

Mugoyo can also be refered to as ingled mashed-sweet potatoes, typically kidney-beans based-simple mixed starchy dish in some communities; exact ingredients vary by region. Can be considered for breakfast also a main cousre dish

Ingredients

  • About 500 grams of dried white but especially red kidney beans(locally known as nambale.)
  • About 500 grams of dark purple sweet potatoes
  • Salt for taste
  • Water (for boiling the beans and steaming the sweet potatoes)
  • Banana leaves (optional, for steaming sweet potatoes)

Instructions

  1. Step1:Soak and then boil the dried beans in salted water until they are tender, which takes the longest time in preparation.
  2. Step2:Peel the sweet potatoes and steam them until soft. Traditionally, the sweet potatoes are wrapped in banana leaves for steaming, which imparts extra flavor.
  3. Step3:Once cooked, mash the sweet potatoes thoroughly.
  4. Step4:Mix the mashed sweet potatoes gently with the cooked beans to form a uniform blend.
  5. Step5:Mugoyo is traditionally roasted or smoked to enhance its flavor, either by roasting wrapped in banana leaves over charcoal or placing it near smouldering fire embers(okubobeeza) for a smoky aroma.
  6. Step6:Serve mugoyo warm, typically as a hearty breakfast accompanied by black tea without milk or sugar to compliment the dry texture of the dish.
